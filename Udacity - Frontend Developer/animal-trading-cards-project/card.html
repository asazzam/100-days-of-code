Moose

The moose is the world’s largest member of the deer family.

  • Scientific Name: Alces alces
  • Average Height: 4.6 – 6.9 ft. (Adult, At Shoulder)
  • Average Weight: 840 – 1,500 lbs (Male), 440 – 1,100 lbs (Female)
  • Average Lifespan: 15 – 25 years

Moose stand out for their solitary nature and striking looks. Majestic in stature with their long legs and, for males, sprawling antlers, they make their home in places like Canada and Alaska. Their diet consists of leaves and bark, and they're surprisingly good swimmers, often diving to find aquatic plants. Moose are also unexpectedly fast for their size, capable of sprinting at speeds up to 35 mph.

Puffin

Puffins can flap their wings up to 400 times per minute and can stay underwater for up to a minute when diving for fish.

  • Species Variety: 4 main species including the Atlantic puffin
  • Average Length: 10 inches
  • Average Weight: 1 lb
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 20 years

Puffins are charming seabirds recognized by their colorful beaks and spirited nature. Adapted for ocean life, they rarely visit land except to nest in lively colonies. Expert divers, puffins use their wings to 'fly' beneath the waves, hunting fish. Their distinctive appearance and underwater agility make them a marvel of coastal ecosystems.

Polar Bear

Polar bears can swim continuously for days, covering distances of over 60 miles without rest.

  • Scientific Name: Ursus maritimus
  • Average Length: 7.9 – 9.8 ft
  • Average Weight: 900 – 1,600 lbs (Male), 400 – 700 lbs (Female)
  • Average Lifespan: 20 – 30 years

Polar bears are the largest land carnivores. With their thick, white fur and powerful build, they are perfectly adapted to the freezing conditions of their habitat. Their solitary nature and skill in hunting seals across the icy expanses underscore their survival in the Arctic's extreme cold.

Eagle

An eagle's vision is 4-8 times stronger than that of the average human, allowing them to spot prey from up to two miles away.

  • Species Variety: Over 60 species across the globe
  • Average Length: 27 – 33 inches
  • Average Weight: 6.6 – 15 lbs
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 30 years

Eagles, with their imposing presence and regal flight, dominate the skies. Known for their sharp talons and powerful beaks, these birds of prey are unmatched hunters. They can achieve speeds of up to 100 miles per hour when diving for prey, showcasing their incredible agility and precision. Eagles build their nests, known as eyries, in high places like cliffs or tall trees.
